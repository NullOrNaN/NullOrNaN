NAME
       coffee-history - historical experiments in infrastructure,
       tooling, and community systems

SYNOPSIS
       man coffee-history

DESCRIPTION
       The coffee-history manual page documents a collection of side
       projects and experiments developed by NullOrNaN while exploring
       infrastructure engineering, automation, and community tooling.

       These projects were often developed in collaboration with other
       engineers and served as practical laboratories for experimenting
       with architecture, operational workflows, and infrastructure
       design.

PROJECTS

       LoungeMC
              Org filled with collaborative projects with Darkefae 
              exploring chat tooling for community game servers. 
              Development focused on ideas around moderation systems, 
              chat architecture, and plugin-based integrations.

              The repository remains available as a reference for the
              design and experimentation conducted during development.

       UploadX
              Community file upload system originally maintained for
              game server infrastructure. The project focused on safe
              file distribution and automated management of uploaded
              assets within community environments.

       drawio-network
              Personal repository containing infrastructure diagrams
              and network topology experiments created using draw.io.

              These diagrams were used to explore rack layouts, network
              architecture, and high-bandwidth infrastructure planning.

       Cloudflare DDNS for UniFi (fork)
              Experimental fork exploring automation for dynamically
              updating DNS records using Cloudflare Workers and UniFi
              network devices.

              The project demonstrates practical experimentation with
              serverless infrastructure and DNS automation.

NOTES
       Many of these projects originated as curiosity-driven
       experiments and were developed alongside professional
       infrastructure work.

       They remain available as references for architecture ideas,
       operational experiments, and collaborative engineering efforts.

BUGS
    Coffee consumption increases with incident severity. Engineers
       are advised to remain within the productive portion of the
       Ballmer Peak curve. Exceeding the optimal threshold may lead to
       spontaneous infrastructure redesign.

       Attempts to debug infrastructure before the first cup of coffee
       may result in false conclusions, misconfigured DNS records, or
       unnecessary service restarts.

       Some experiments documented here may remain permanently in the
       "interesting idea" phase.

       Certain diagrams in drawio-network may reflect late-night
       architectural optimism rather than production reality.

       DNS issues may persist even when they are clearly not DNS.

       Collaboration with other engineers may result in new ideas,
       additional complexity, or spontaneous side projects.

       Prolonged exposure to infrastructure tooling may cause the
       author to redesign home networks unnecessarily.

       Some solutions documented here were discovered after first
       attempting to fix the problem the wrong way.

       Debugging success rate is inversely proportional to the number
       of people watching the terminal.

       Incident timelines reconstructed from memory may be subject
       to caffeine-related inaccuracies.

       Wait... where is my coffee?

       The phrase "this should be simple" has historically preceded
       several multi-hour debugging sessions.
